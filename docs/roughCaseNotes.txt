Tender price vs best bid or ask

Do not warehouse inventory

Liability Trading Case
Every strategy must explicitly answer:
Who am I in this market?
 Role definition
Liability trader at an investment bank facilitating institutional orders.


What is my objective?
 Execution goal, not price prediction
Make profit only on the spread between the tender offer price and the institutional price, while facilitating institutional orders.
Where does profit come from?
 Spread, arbitrage, or fee differentials
Comes from the difference between market price and the price offered by the institution. Also known as liquidity spread.


What are the risks?
 Liquidity, inventory, fines, execution
4 types of penalties:
Front-Running: you see the tender offer, and trade in the market before you have accepted the offer (insider trading)
Solution: Always trade after a tender offer has been accepted. Never outside of when you have an open position from accepting the tender offer.
Market Maker Activity: No making profits off the bid and ask.
Solution: make sure correct algo is connected via API. Separate scripting logic for liability trading and market making algo.
Speculation: Random trades which are trying to predict which side the market is going.
Solution: Avoid it completely. Only trade to cover the accepted tender offer positions. 
End of Session Uncovered position -> $1 per share fine for any outstanding shares at the end of the 300 second simulation.
Solution: Add logic to the script that closes all outstanding positions 5 seconds before the end of the session. Accepting a small loss on these positions is fine because it is better than getting the fine.
Liquidity risk -> your orders are not filled
Market risk -> market goes against you after you have accepted tender offer


How are risks mitigated?
 Rules, limits, automation logic
For fines, make sure that algo is only trading within the constraints of the case (after a tender offer has been accepted), it has a flattening function to close all open positions before end of simulation
For liquidity risk -> entry rule to accept the offer form institution, are there are certain number greater than that on the opposite side available in the market?
For market risk -> key assumption we don’t knwo where teh market will go, and we are not trying to predict it, we will base the accepting of the offer on the current liquidity spread between the order and the liquidity spread, and use trailing stop loss logic to maximize profit, we will also wait for teh entire 30 seconds to determine if the offer is worth taking or not
